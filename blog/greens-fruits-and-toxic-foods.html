Greens, Fruits, & Toxic Foods

March 9, 2026

Introduction

Pigeons are mainly granivores, meaning that they should be on a diet of primarily seeds. Though vegetables and fruits should never be a part of their main diet, studies show that pigeons and doves in the wild will usually eat grains and seeds, with occasional intake of berries and some other plants! While pigeons are primarily granivorous, research shows that dietary diversity and plant-derived nutrients play a role in avian health. Leafy greens and vegetables provide carotenoids, vitamins, and minerals that support immune function, metabolism, and tissue health.

It is a myth that small amounts of vegetables or fruits will kill or harm your bird. This is a myth that is widely spread in the pet pigeon community, especially with little evidence of vegetables and fruits harming small amounts. In fact, evidence shows that small amounts of vegetables can even be beneficial for pigeons! We recommend treating vegetables as enrichment tools, but they should not be more than. Fruits, however, are very high in sugar, which can be damaging to your bird's health, and should be only offered in moderation as treats. Regardless, fruits and vegetables should be finely chopped or minced for ease of ingestion. We recommend offering vegetables up to 3 or 4 times per week, but never daily.

We do not recommend going above 10% vegetables of weekly food intake. Fruits are treats only.


Vegetables & Legumes

The following vegetables and legumes are considered safe for pigeons.

  • Leafy Greens: mustard greens, collard greens, turnip greens, beet greens, watercress, romaine lettuce, arugula, kale, spinach
  • Legumes: cooked - lentils, chickpeas, split peas; sprouted lentils or mung beans, green peas
  • Other Veggies: zucchini, cucumber, pumpkin, green beans, okra, green or red cabbage, cooked sweet potato, bell peppers

Out of these, you can use greens, sprouts, and leafy vegetables as enrichment tools. Pigeons don't have strong beaks, and they can be a bit easier for them to tear apart than other vegetables. Note that all sources of veggies should not add sweeteners when canned. Purees of different veggies can be offered so long as there's no added sugar.

Pigeons should not eat most of the food on your plate! However, in a post made by u/blueyurble on Reddit, the spinach is unseasoned with no dressing, making it safe.


Fruits

As stated before, fruits should be treated as treats. The following fruits are safe for pigeons. In general, avoid feeding ANY fruit seeds or pits to pigeons.

  • Berries: strawberries, blueberries, blackberries, raspberries
  • Pome fruits (NO SEEDS): apples, pears
  • Stone Fruits (NO PITS): peaches, nectarines, plums, apricots, cherries, mango
  • Citrus Fruits (occasionally; acidity can affect digestion): oranges, mandarins, tangerines, clementines
  • Melons: watermelon, cantaloupe, honeydew
  • Other Fruits: bananas, kiwi, lychee, dragon fruit, rambutan

Toxic Foods

There are a plethora of toxic foods for pigeons and birds alike. Among these are avocado, chocolate, caffeine products, alliums, fruit seeds and pits, alcohol, high salt foods, dairy products, and sugary or artificially sweetened foods.

Avocados are a known toxic food to most animals. The fruit, skin, and pit are all toxic. Avocados contain persin, a fungicidal toxin that causes severe cardiovascular damage, respiratory distress, and eventually death. The effects are rapid, and often the bird can pass anywhere from a few days to less than a day.

Chocolate contains theobromine and caffeine. Birds cannot break down methylxanthines (theobromine and caffeine), and with the high metabolic rates and sensitive heart rates, stimulants harm them easily, causing overstimulation to the central nervous system and cardiovascular system.

Alliums include things like onions, garlic, shallots, chives, and leeks. These contain sulfur compounds that cause oxidative damage to red blood cells, triggering hemolysis (destruction of red blood cells). Small amounts can cause respiratory distress, weakness, and acute anemia, all of which can lead to death. Studies show that as little of 0.5% of body weight ingested in alliums can be harmful. That's about 2 grams ingested for the average 400g bird.

The seeds of apples and pears, along with the pits of other fruits, contain cyanogenic glycosides. In short, these release cyanide when ingested, and may cause cyanide poisoning when consumed in an amount too large.

Alcoholic beverages are dangerous as pigeons cannot break down ethanol, leading to respiratory failure, disorientation, and death. If the bird hasn't ingested enough to kill, a mix of neurological damage and organ damage can combine to weaken the bird till death.

Other high salt processed foods, candies, foods with extra sugar, or foods with artificial sweeteners can also harm birds. Some sweeteners may also be toxic.
