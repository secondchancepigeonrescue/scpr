Household Hazards To Pigeons

March 11, 2026

Introduction

One big switch many new pigeon owners have to make is the switch from things present in their day-to-day life. There are likely dangers present in your home that are considered dangerous to many birds (we'll give you seasoned pigeon and parrot owners alike some credit). Birds have very sensitive respiratory systems. Things that are harmless to us in smaller amounts can be deadly to pigeons. In addition, many things that are usually considered safe or low risk for other pets can be incredibly dangerous to pigeons. To note, pigeons can't usually chew on plants, but a determined pigeon might be able to tear and break off pieces with some consistent effort.


Hazards In The Air

  • PTFE/Teflon coated cookware items (pots, pans, waffle makers), space heaters, iron or ironing boards, and hair tools
  • Aerosol sprays, including air fresheners, spray cleaners, hairspray, and spray-on deodorants
  • Scented products, including candles, wax welts, incense, perfumes, and body sprays
  • Smoke from cigarettes, vapes, fireplaces, or burnt food
  • Strong cleaners like bleach, ammonia, and oven cleaner. While you may be able to use these in your home, the solution should be very diluted and used away from your bird.

One of the largest concerns of the list is PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene), including Teflon, coated products, as it's one that many people don't realize is even an issue until it's too late, and is known to be one of the largest causes of bird death in a home. The PTFE coating begins to degrade and releases fumes when heated to 500°F or 260°C, though it's been noted to be caused at a lesser temperature, from 460°F or 250°C. When inhaled by birds, it can quickly cause respiratory distress and neurological symptoms. Perivascular edema (swelling around the blood vessels) has been noted, leading to hemorrhage (breakage or damage to blood vessels causing heavy bleeding). According to a study done regarding PTFE toxicosis in young chicks, "Overheating of nonstick cookware is a known cause of PTFE toxicosis in avian species and has often been reported anecdotally in pet birds in the veterinary clinical literature."


Toxic Houseplants

It's argued on whether pigeons mess with plants or not, or pigeons can even successfully chew or tear plants. In short, yes, pigeons can tear off pieces of plants given enough time and effort. However, many pigeons will simply ignore houseplants or not tear them at all. We advise owners to avoid allowing pigeons near houseplants that are toxic if they notice them pecking and tearing.

  • Houseplants: philodendron, dumb cane, pothos, calla lillies, peace lillies, snake plants, monstera, croton, schefflera
  • Flowers: azalea, foxglove, holly, hydrangea, morning glory, iris, larkspur, chrysanthemum
  • Other: tobacco, shamrock, rhubarb, oleander, mistletoe, yew

For a full list, take a look at Niles Animal Hospital and Bird Medical Center's file on Toxic Plants for Pet Birds.


Physical Hazards

  • Windows and large mirrors may be crashed into
  • Ceiling fans when turned on can kill a bird why tries to fly around or into them
  • Hot stoves and pots; don't let your bird out while cooking
  • Loose screen doors/windows, or doors/windows left open leading outside
  • Other pets, specifically cats, dogs, and ferrets who are known to be predatory

Easily Ingested Items

Beware of items left out in the open that pigeons can ingest easily. Many of these can cause blockages in the gut, requiring surgery to remove which is not only dangerous but expensive. Lead specifically is very toxic to pigeons.

  • Lead weights
  • Small jewelry
  • Small plactic pieces
  • Thread or string
