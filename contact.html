Contact us via email or by using the contact form below. We welcome inquiries regarding adoption, fostering, support, or general questions regarding pigeon care, etc.! Response time may take up to a week, but look out for an email response within 24-48 hours. We appreciate your patience.

I found a pigeon. What do I do?

If the pigeon is on the ground or visible, easy to approach and catch, it could be sick or injured. Get the bird somewhere safe. Catch it as soon as possible. If you don't have a towel or shirt on hand, you can use your hands. Ensure proper washing afterwards. Keep the wings against its body and attempt to wrap both hands around it. Pigeons have stronger feet than you may realize, and can wriggle out!

Put the bird in a box or carrier with a blanket or towel underneath for comfort. Paper towels or puppy pads are fine to use. Bring the bird indoors and keep it dark and quiet to reduce stress.

Contact us immediately after capture. Pigeons can hide injury and illness well. By the time symptoms start, the clock begins ticking down to save it! We also recommend posting on the Palomacy Help Group for Pigeon & Dove Rescue & Adoption.


What if I have cats, dogs, or other birds?


If you have dogs or cats, keep them away from the area. Other birds can not only get sick, but transfer illness to the pigeon. Preferably, keep the carrier or box in a bathroom, closet, or other area where your other pets can't reach. Practice good biosecurity measures and deep clean your hands and change clothes to prevent this.


What if the bird has bands?


Bands that say "PET" or something similar can be a lost pet! However, other bands are more common. It may include a number of colored bands without numbers or letters, or have AU, IF, CU, NBRC, or some form of local club code. These are racing pigeons. Racers often do not respond to rescues or do not care about their birds. Volunteers from other pigeons rescues have driven hours to return birds, only for the racer to cull the bird in front of volunteers. For that reason, we do not return racing birds to racing owners.


Other Inquiries

Can I surrender my pigeon to you?


We do not currently accept owner surrenders. Our goal is to rehabilitate injured and sick pigeons at the moment due to space limitations. In the future, we do plan to accept owner surrenders from owners in situations that require surrenders. We do not accept birds from breeders unless the bird requires rehabilitation, and the bird will not be returned.


I found a bird that isn't a pigeon. Can I deliver it to you anyways?


We do not accept any other species of birds besides domesticated rock pigeons. If you've found another species of domesticated bird, please contact another rescue. If you've found a wild bird in need of help, please locate a wildlife rehabilitator near you via your state's wildlife department.
